The Beckham Law: Your Exclusive Tax Route

The Beckham Law is a special tax regime in Spain designed to attract international executives, entrepreneurs and highly-skilled professionals. Qualified newcomers can benefit from a 24% flat income tax rate on employment earnings up to €600,000, with taxation limited to Spanish-source income. This exclusive framework simplifies Spanish residency and is available for six years when strict eligibility criteria are met, offering a streamlined route for those relocating to Spain【917969817622208†screenshot】.

Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for the Beckham Law you must not have been tax resident in Spain during the previous five years and must relocate because of a genuine job move. Eligible scenarios include signing a new employment contract with a Spanish company, being assigned to a Spanish entity by your current employer, working remotely from Spain for a foreign company, becoming a director of a Spanish company (without owning a significant stake), launching an innovative start‑up or carrying out research or training activities【756971887955324†screenshot】【47338107579236†screenshot】. The application must be filed within six months of starting your activity, and income must not be earned through a permanent establishment【92456647547442†screenshot】.

Fiscal Benefits

The regime taxes employment income at a flat 24% up to €600,000 and 47% on any excess【111005362038336†screenshot】. Only income generated in Spain is subject to taxation, while foreign earnings remain exempt【917969817622208†screenshot】. Wealth and solidarity taxes apply solely to assets located in Spain【664065058939297†screenshot】, and beneficiaries are relieved from filing the Modelo 720 foreign assets return【111005362038336†screenshot】. Note that pension contributions and other remuneration in kind remain fully taxable and there is no deduction for property expenses, so careful planning is essential【128184904148107†screenshot】.

Duration & Renewals

The Beckham Law applies for the tax year in which you become a resident plus the following five years, making a total of six years of preferential treatment【917969817622208†screenshot】. You must submit your application within six months of starting your professional activity【756971887955324†screenshot】. The regime terminates if you cease to meet the requirements, leave Spain, or once the six‑year period expires【804495120209323†screenshot】. Each year you must file the special non‑resident tax return and evaluate whether remaining in the regime continues to be advantageous.

Costs & Exclusions

Although the flat rate is attractive, the Beckham regime does not allow deductions for expenses related to Spanish property, and certain types of income remain fully taxable. Severance payments, public pensions, scholarships, disability benefits and annuities are not exempt under this regime【128184904148107†screenshot】. Rental income is taxed without the 60% reduction available to residents【664065058939297†screenshot】, and contributions to Spanish pension plans are taxed at 24% regardless of amount【128184904148107†screenshot】. There is no entitlement to personal allowances or deductions, so the overall tax burden may be higher for some profiles.

Procedure & Timeline

The application process begins with a preliminary assessment to ensure the regime is worthwhile and to verify deadlines【250560986740036†screenshot】. You must register with the Spanish Tax Agency, obtain a foreigner identification number and submit Form 030, then file your application electronically with supporting documents within six months【756971887955324†screenshot】. The tax agency may request additional information before issuing a resolution. Once accepted, you must file the special tax return annually, assess wealth or solidarity tax obligations and stay informed of legislative changes【250560986740036†screenshot】. Termination can occur voluntarily, through non‑compliance or when the six-year period ends【804495120209323†screenshot】.

Compliance & Risks

Maintaining eligibility requires adherence to strict rules: you cannot operate a permanent establishment in Spain, must continue to perform your qualifying professional activity and must not acquire tax residency through other criteria【47338107579236†screenshot】. The regime may not suit those with significant foreign passive income or property income due to the absence of deductions and credits【128184904148107†screenshot】. Non‑compliance or missing the six‑month application window results in loss of the regime【756971887955324†screenshot】. Professional advice is recommended to navigate these rules and ensure continued compliance【250560986740036†screenshot】.

Special Cases & FAQs

Spouses and children under 25 who move with the main applicant can opt into the Beckham regime provided they also meet the five‑year non‑residency requirement and do not earn income through a permanent establishment【47338107579236†screenshot】. Each additional family member enjoys the same tax rate and limits, though all must file individual applications【804495120209323†screenshot】. Frequently asked questions involve how teleworking arrangements fit within the rules, the treatment of stock options and the interaction with wealth taxes. These points underscore the importance of personalised advice tailored to your circumstances.
